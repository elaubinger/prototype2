Alternating Turns Prototype

Pitch Doc: The Fantastic Abyss

High Concept:
Final Fantasy Tactics-like combat in a Roguelike dungeon. Players control a team of warriors as they descend into the ever-growing challenge beneath.


Genre:

Turn-based strategy RPG.


Gameplay:

Players outfit a party with RPG style equipment (weapons, armor, gadgets) in between turn based encounters. The turn based battles are controlled in a Final Fantasy Tactics-like style with each character taking a turn in order. The player controls characters in their party and uses a variety of combat styles to overcome enemies and traps.


Features:

  • Turn based RPG combat

  • Randomized dungeons and enemies

  • Large amount of customization

  • Strategic depth

  • Generous variety

  • Replayability


Setting:

A large town surrounds the labyrinth, whose deepest depths have never been seen. Many come from far and wide to explore it's inner reaches; however, few possess the courage to strike deep into the maze. Red banners fly and trumpets sound heralding the arrival of a new party plunging to their almost certain doom: perhaps they are the King's men on a holy mission, or prisoners sentenced to death, or perhaps something even more bizarre. Whatever the case, the abyss makes all equal in the end.


Story:

The loose story as referenced in the setting is you command a party of adventurers attempting to crawl as deep as possible into a mysterious, seemingly endless dungeon.


Target Audience:

Hardcore computer enthusiasts.


Hardware Platforms:

Summary:

Tactics games are enormous fun as are Roguelike games. This pairing of the two would see the best parts of customization, choice, and random-elements from Roguelike games pair with the strategic depth, planning, and multiple-characters of a tactics game to form something different while preserving the best parts of both.
